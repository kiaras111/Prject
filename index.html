S1 | What is Gender Sterotype?

Gender Stereotype is also known as Sexism. It is a form of oppression where certain groups of people are treated differently and unfairly based on their gender. It can be seen in many institutions, including schools, workplaces, and the media. Gender stereotypes can lead to gender inequality and discrimination.

When and how did it start?

Gender Stereotype started back in ancient times around, possibly in 1800s even. was believed that men and women had different roles in society, and that men were supposed to be strong and assertive while women were supposed to be gentle and passive. These gender roles have been passed down through generations and are still present in many cultures today.
